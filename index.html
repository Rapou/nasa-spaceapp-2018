welcome to the solar system orchestra

What if the solar system would be a kind of sequencer and each planet an instrument?

We worked on a proposition to listen to an interpretation of this symphony.

To achieve this goal, we created a signature sound for each planet and defined a system by using static and dynamic data from Nasa. Here is our process:

- Colors to keys (Static).
Determine the 3 dominant colors for each planet, 3 colors = 3 keys = a chord (based on a wavelength conversion from nm to Hz, it defines a key, then each result is confronted with Charles Blanc-Gatti theory from the artistic movement called the Musicalism).

- Dimension of the planet defines the octave from which the keys are played (Static).

- Rotation speed variation of each planet affects a fine tuning on each key (Static).

- Orbital speed of each planet around the sun is connected to the low pass filter (Dynamic).

- Distance from each planet to earth defines the volume’s variations and the panning (Dynamic).

- Asteroids sounds are defined by their distance from earth and magnitude and their total number per day appears randomly.

As scale, 6 minutes here represents the full orbit of Neptune around the sun that takes approximately 165 year in earth time.

The result is a powerful and spiritual music which reminds to the overtone singing and the repetitive music and experimental sound of artists such as Terry Riley or the master of the phasing music: Steve Reich.
